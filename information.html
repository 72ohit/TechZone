Component Glossary

Understanding the jargon can be half the battle in PC building. Here's a quick guide to some common terms you'll encounter:

  • **CPU Socket:** The part of the motherboard that holds the CPU. Must match your CPU's socket type (e.g., AM5, LGA1700).
  • **RAM (Random Access Memory):** Volatile memory used for temporary data storage, crucial for multitasking and application performance. Look for DDR4 or DDR5 for modern builds.
  • **PCIe (Peripheral Component Interconnect Express):** A high-speed expansion slot used for graphics cards, SSDs, and other expansion cards. Different generations (PCIe 3.0, 4.0, 5.0) offer varying bandwidths.
  • **NVMe SSD:** A type of Solid State Drive that connects via PCIe, offering significantly faster read/write speeds than traditional SATA SSDs.
  • **PSU (Power Supply Unit):** Converts AC power from the wall into DC power needed by your PC components. Ensure adequate wattage for your build.
  • **TDP (Thermal Design Power):** The maximum amount of heat generated by a computer chip (like a CPU) that the cooling system is designed to dissipate. Helps in choosing appropriate coolers.

For a complete list and in-depth explanations, refer to our comprehensive buying guides.
